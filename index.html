Adding and Subtracting Like Fractions

What are Like Fractions?

Like fractions are fractions that have the same denominators (bottom numbers).

Adding Like Fractions

  1. Check the Denominators: Make sure the denominators (bottom numbers) are the same.
  2. Add the Numerators: Add the top numbers (numerators) together.
  3. Keep the Denominator the Same: Keep the bottom number (denominator) the same.

Example: Adding 1/5 + 2/5

  1. Denominators are the same (both are 5).
  2. Add the numerators: 1 + 2 = 3.
  3. Keep the denominator the same: 5.

So, 1/5 + 2/5 = 3/5.

Subtracting Like Fractions

  1. Check the Denominators: Make sure the denominators (bottom numbers) are the same.
  2. Subtract the Numerators: Subtract the top numbers (numerators).
  3. Keep the Denominator the Same: Keep the bottom number (denominator) the same.

Example: Subtracting 2/5 - 1/5

  1. Denominators are the same (both are 5).
  2. Subtract the numerators: 2 - 1 = 1
  3. Keep the denominator the same: 5.

So, 2/5 - 1/5 = 1/5.

Reducing Fractions

What is Reducing a Fraction?

  • Reducing a fraction means making it as simple as possible.

How to Reduce Fractions

Method 1: Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

  1. Find Factors: List all the numbers that can divide the numerator (top number) and the denominator (bottom number) without leaving a remainder.
    For example: For 24/32, list factors:
    • Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
    • Factors of 32: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32
  2. Find the Greatest Common Factor (GCF): Find the largest number that appears in both lists.
    • Here, the GCF of 24 and 32 is 8.
  3. Divide: Divide the numerator and denominator by the GCF.
    • 24/8 = 3
    • 32/8 = 4

So, 24/32 reduces to 3/4.



Method 2: Keep Dividing by Small Numbers

  1. Pick a Small Number: Choose a number like 2, 3, 4, 5, or 7.
    • Example: For 24/32, use 2.
  2. Divide: Divide the numerator and denominator by the small number.
    • 24/2 = 12
    • 32/2 = 16
    • The new fraction is 12/16.
  3. Repeat: Keep dividing until you can’t divide anymore.
    • 12/2 = 6
    • 16/2 = 8
    • The new fraction is 6/8.
    • 6/2 = 3
    • 8/2 = 4
    • The final reduced fraction is 3/4.

Method 3: Prime Factorization

  1. Find Prime Factors: Break down the numerator and denominator into prime numbers.

    Example: For 60/84:

    • Prime factors of 60: 2 × 2 × 3 × 5
    • Prime factors of 84: 2 × 2 × 3 × 7
  2. Cancel Common Factors: Cross out the common factors.
    • Common factors: 2 × 2 × 3 = 12
  3. Divide: Divide the numerator and denominator by the product of the common factors.
    • 60/12 = 5
    • 84/12 = 7
    • The final reduced fraction is 5/7.
